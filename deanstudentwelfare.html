Prof. Jasminder Singh

Dean Student Welfare, University of North West Himalayas (UNWH)

Prof. Jasminder Singh is a compassionate and dynamic leader with over 22 years of experience in student development, mentoring, and welfare administration. As Dean Student Welfare at UNWH, he is the guardian of student life — ensuring every student feels supported, safe, included, and empowered throughout their university journey.

Prof. Singh oversees a wide range of student-centric initiatives including hostel administration, counseling services, career guidance, scholarships & financial aid, grievance redressal, anti-ragging measures, health & wellness programs, sports & cultural activities, and student clubs & societies. His focus is on creating a nurturing, inclusive, and vibrant campus environment where every student can thrive personally and academically.

Under his leadership, UNWH has launched several impactful programs such as: 24×7 student helpline, peer mentoring system, mental health awareness workshops, leadership development camps, gender sensitization drives, skill enhancement sessions, and annual student welfare festivals. He has also strengthened the student council, making it a true voice of the student community.

Prof. Singh holds a Ph.D. in [relevant field – e.g., Psychology / Education / Management] and is trained in student counseling, conflict resolution, and youth development. He has been actively involved in national student welfare forums and has received recognition for his contributions to student well-being and campus harmony.

He strongly believes that student welfare is the heart of any great university. His approach is rooted in empathy, accessibility, and proactive support — ensuring no student is left behind due to personal, financial, emotional, or social challenges. He works tirelessly to make UNWH a second home where students feel valued, respected, and motivated to achieve their dreams.

"A university is only as strong as the happiness and growth of its students. My mission is to create a campus where every student feels heard, cared for, and inspired to become the best version of themselves."

With genuine care and commitment,
Prof. Jasminder Singh
